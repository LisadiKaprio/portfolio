### What is it about?

In this game, the player can scroll through a tiny portion of a city filled with houses, with some of the windows in the buildings being lit up. Clicking on those reveals a snapshot of the family's life there.

::lightbox-images{:items='["what-are-the-lives-2"]'}
::

### What was the goal?

I joined a group of students at my university, that each worked either in teams or on their own to create games and interactive experiences to make others aware of the ongoing war in Ukraine and to educate others on certain aspects of it. I had an urge to communicate the tragedy that comes with one's everyday life being suddenly interrupted by an enormous tragedy, without them ever expecting it.

### Art assets and lessons learned

::lightbox-images{:items='["what-are-the-lives-5.jpg", "what-are-the-lives-6.jpg"]'}
::
In this project, I got the experience of conveying destruction of a lived-in location. I thought of how to draw an environment that feels like people live in it, and how then to convey the tragedy and destruction in this location. I find that I didn't convey these two aspects of the scene to the full extent, and look forward to any new projects where I can tackle those aspects again.

I pursued the goal of conveying a message about a tragic and serious matter, while picking an artstyle that eases the player into it and makes them relate to the characters, letting them see parallels between their own day-to-day life and the lives presented in the game. This means I had to play around with different ideas for artstyle, but also had to keep in mind the tight schedule and thus limit the scope.
